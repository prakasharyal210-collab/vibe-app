PANCHANGA (HINDU ALMANAC) — 5 ELEMENTS OF EACH DAY:
Every day in Vedic astrology has 5 elements that determine if it is auspicious or inauspicious:

Tithi (Lunar Day) — 30 tithis in a lunar month

Pratipada (1st) to Amavasya (New Moon) and Purnima (Full Moon)
Each tithi has a ruling deity and specific activities to avoid/do
Amavasya (New Moon) — ancestor worship, avoid new beginnings
Purnima (Full Moon) — most auspicious, fasting and worship


Vara (Day of Week) — each day ruled by a planet

Sunday (Ravivar) — Sun — good for government, authority work
Monday (Somvar) — Moon — good for travel, water activities, Shiva worship
Tuesday (Mangalvar) — Mars — avoid new ventures, good for courage activities
Wednesday (Budhvar) — Mercury — good for business, education, communication
Thursday (Guruvar) — Jupiter — most auspicious day, good for all new beginnings
Friday (Shukravar) — Venus — good for love, arts, buying luxury items
Saturday (Shanivar) — Saturn — avoid new beginnings, good for discipline work


Nakshatra (Star of the day) — Moon's position in 27 Nakshatras

Each Nakshatra has specific auspicious and inauspicious activities


Yoga (Planetary Combination) — 27 Yogas

Vishkumbha to Vaidhriti
Some are auspicious (Siddha, Shubha, Shukla)
Some are inauspicious (Vishkumbha, Atiganda, Shoola)


Karana (Half Lunar Day) — 11 Karanas

Bava, Balava, Kaulava, Taitila, Gara, Vanija, Vishti (Bhadra — very inauspicious)




MUHURTA (AUSPICIOUS TIMING):
Muhurta is the science of choosing the perfect time for important activities:

Vivah Muhurta — marriage ceremony timing
Griha Pravesh Muhurta — entering new home
Naamkaran Muhurta — baby naming ceremony
Vidyarambha Muhurta — starting education
Vyapar Muhurta — starting business
Yatra Muhurta — starting a journey

Most auspicious Muhurtas:

Brahma Muhurta — 1.5 hours before sunrise, best for meditation and spiritual work
Abhijit Muhurta — midday, universally auspicious (except Wednesday)
Amrit Siddhi Yoga — when specific Nakshatra meets specific day

Inauspicious times to avoid:

Rahu Kaal — 1.5 hour inauspicious period each day (different time each day)
Yamagandam — inauspicious period associated with Yama (god of death)
Gulika Kaal — inauspicious period associated with Saturn's son
Durmuhurta — two inauspicious periods each day


SPECIAL ASTROLOGICAL EVENTS:

Solar Eclipse (Surya Grahan) — Rahu swallows Sun. Very powerful. Avoid eating, chanting mantras amplified 1000x
Lunar Eclipse (Chandra Grahan) — Rahu swallows Moon. Affects mind and emotions. Powerful time for mantras
Mercury Retrograde — Budha appears to move backward. Avoid signing contracts, new communication ventures
Jupiter Retrograde — Guru turns inward. Good for spiritual study, inner wisdom
Saturn Retrograde (Shani Vakri) — Karmic delays return. Old lessons resurface
Venus Retrograde — Shukra turns inward. Avoid new relationships, luxury purchases


SPECIAL DASHAS AND PERIODS:

Shani Sade Sati — Saturn's 7.5 year transit. Three phases:

Rising (12th from Moon) — mental pressure, expenses
Peak (1st from Moon) — physical challenges, identity crisis
Setting (2nd from Moon) — financial pressure, family issues


Shani Dhaiya (Kantaka Shani) — Saturn transiting 4th or 8th from Moon. 2.5 years of challenges
Rahu/Ketu Transit — Every 18 months they change signs. Causes sudden changes and karmic events
Jupiter Transit (Guru Peyarchi) — Every year Jupiter changes sign. Brings new opportunities and growth to certain Rashis
