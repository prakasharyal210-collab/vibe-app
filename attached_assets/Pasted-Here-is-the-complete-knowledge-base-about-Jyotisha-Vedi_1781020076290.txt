WHAT IS JYOTISHA:
Jyotisha (ज्योतिष) is one of the six Vedangas — the six limbs of the Vedas, the oldest scriptures of Hinduism. The word Jyotisha comes from Sanskrit "Jyoti" meaning light — it is literally the science of light and cosmic time. It is considered a divine gift from the gods to help humans understand their karma and dharma.
Jyotisha is NOT fortune telling. It is a spiritual science that maps the exact position of planets and stars at the moment of a person's birth to reveal:

Their past karma (actions from previous lives)
Their current dharma (life purpose and duties)
The timing of major life events
Their innate nature, strengths and weaknesses
The path to moksha (spiritual liberation)

ORIGIN OF JYOTISHA:
Jyotisha originated in ancient India over 5,000 years ago. Its roots are in the Rigveda, the oldest of the four Vedas. The sage Lagadha wrote the first systematic text on Jyotisha called "Vedanga Jyotisha" around 1400 BCE.
The classical texts of Jyotisha include:

Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra — the most important text, written by sage Parashara, father of Vyasa (author of Mahabharata). This is the foundation of all Vedic astrology.
Brihat Jataka — written by Varahamihira (505-587 CE), one of the nine gems of Vikramaditya's court
Saravali — written by Kalyanvarma
Phaladeepika — written by Mantreswara
Jataka Parijata — written by Vaidyanatha Dikshita
Hora Sara — written by Prithuyasas

The knowledge was passed from the gods to sages in meditation. Lord Brahma (creator) gave this knowledge to sage Narada, who gave it to sage Parashara, who compiled it into Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra.

HOW THE JYOTISHA ALGORITHM WORKS:
STEP 1 — THE BIRTH CHART (KUNDALI/JANMA PATRIKA):
The birth chart is calculated using:

Exact date of birth
Exact time of birth (accurate to the minute)
Exact place of birth (latitude and longitude)

The chart is a map of the sky at the exact moment of birth. It shows where each of the 9 planets (Navagraha) was positioned in the 12 zodiac signs (Rashis) and 12 houses (Bhavas).
STEP 2 — THE 12 RASHIS (ZODIAC SIGNS):
Unlike Western astrology which uses the Sun sign, Vedic astrology primarily uses the Moon sign (Chandra Rashi) and the Ascendant (Lagna).
The 12 Rashis are:

Mesha (Aries) — Mars ruled — fire sign — courage, leadership
Vrishabha (Taurus) — Venus ruled — earth sign — stability, wealth
Mithuna (Gemini) — Mercury ruled — air sign — intelligence, communication
Karka (Cancer) — Moon ruled — water sign — emotions, home, mother
Simha (Leo) — Sun ruled — fire sign — royalty, authority, ego
Kanya (Virgo) — Mercury ruled — earth sign — service, analysis, health
Tula (Libra) — Venus ruled — air sign — balance, relationships, beauty
Vrishchika (Scorpio) — Mars/Ketu ruled — water sign — transformation, secrets
Dhanu (Sagittarius) — Jupiter ruled — fire sign — wisdom, philosophy, travel
Makara (Capricorn) — Saturn ruled — earth sign — discipline, karma, career
Kumbha (Aquarius) — Saturn/Rahu ruled — air sign — innovation, humanity
Meena (Pisces) — Jupiter/Ketu ruled — water sign — spirituality, dreams, moksha

STEP 3 — THE LAGNA (ASCENDANT):
The Lagna is the zodiac sign rising on the eastern horizon at the exact moment of birth. It changes every 2 hours, which is why birth time must be exact. The Lagna represents:

The physical body and appearance
Personality and how others see you
Overall life direction
The filter through which all planets express themselves

The Lagna is considered more important than the Sun sign in Vedic astrology.
STEP 4 — THE 27 NAKSHATRAS (LUNAR MANSIONS):
The Moon travels through 27 Nakshatras (star clusters) in its monthly cycle. Each Nakshatra spans 13°20' of the zodiac. The Nakshatra where the Moon is placed at birth is called the Janma Nakshatra and is extremely important.

STEP 5 — THE 9 NAVAGRAHA (PLANETS):
Vedic astrology uses 9 celestial bodies called Grahas (seizers). Each Graha represents a specific type of karma:

Surya (Sun) — Soul, father, authority, government, health, ego. King of planets. Represents the Atma (soul). Day: Sunday. Color: Red/Orange. Gemstone: Ruby.
Chandra (Moon) — Mind, mother, emotions, water, fertility, public. Queen of planets. Represents Manas (mind). Changes sign every 2.5 days. Day: Monday. Color: White. Gemstone: Pearl.
Mangal (Mars) — Energy, courage, aggression, brothers, land, military, accidents. Commander of planets. Day: Tuesday. Color: Red. Gemstone: Red Coral.
Budha (Mercury) — Intelligence, communication, commerce, nervous system, skin. Prince of planets. Day: Wednesday. Color: Green. Gemstone: Emerald.
Guru/Brihaspati (Jupiter) — Wisdom, dharma, children, wealth, spirituality, teacher, expansion. Teacher of the gods (Devaguru). Day: Thursday. Color: Yellow. Gemstone: Yellow Sapphire.
Shukra (Venus) — Love, beauty, luxury, arts, marriage, vehicles, eyes. Teacher of demons (Asura Guru). Day: Friday. Color: White/Pink. Gemstone: Diamond.
Shani (Saturn) — Karma, discipline, obstacles, delays, longevity, servants, old age. Slowest planet — stays 2.5 years in each sign. Son of Sun. Day: Saturday. Color: Blue/Black. Gemstone: Blue Sapphire.
Rahu (North Node) — Desire, illusion, foreigners, technology, obsession, worldly ambition. Shadow planet — no physical body. Causes solar/lunar eclipses. Represents material desire and Maya (illusion).
Ketu (South Node) — Past life karma, spirituality, liberation, mysticism, isolation. Shadow planet — opposite to Rahu. Represents spiritual liberation and detachment. Always exactly opposite Rahu.

STEP 8 — PLANETARY STRENGTH (SHADBALA):
Planets gain or lose strength based on:

STEP 9 — DASHA SYSTEM (PLANETARY PERIODS):
The Vimshottari Dasha system is the most important timing system in Jyotisha. Total cycle = 120 years.
Each planet rules a period:

Ketu Dasha — 7 years
Venus Dasha — 20 years
Sun Dasha — 6 years
Moon Dasha — 10 years
Mars Dasha — 7 years
Rahu Dasha — 18 years
Jupiter Dasha — 16 years
Saturn Dasha — 19 years
Mercury Dasha — 17 years

The starting Dasha is determined by the Moon's Nakshatra at birth. Each main Dasha (Mahadasha) is divided into sub-periods (Antardasha) of each planet.
During a planet's Dasha, that planet's energy dominates life events. A strong benefic planet's Dasha brings growth and blessings. A weak or malefic planet's Dasha brings challenges and lessons.
STEP 10 — TRANSITS (GOCHAR):
Planetary transits show current planetary positions and their effect on the birth chart. Most important transits:

Shani (Saturn) transit — stays 2.5 years per sign, most impactful
Guru (Jupiter) transit — stays 1 year per sign, brings expansion
Rahu/Ketu transit — stays 18 months per sign, causes major changes

SADE SATI — Saturn's 7.5 year transit through the 12th, 1st, and 2nd houses from Moon sign. Most challenging period in life. Tests karma. Teaches humility and discipline.
STEP 11 — YOGAS (SPECIAL COMBINATIONS):
Yogas are special planetary combinations that give unique results:
Benefic Yogas (bring good results):

Raj Yoga — planets in kendras (1,4,7,10) or trikonas (1,5,9) forming conjunction or exchange. Gives power, status, success.
Dhana Yoga — 2nd and 11th house lords connected. Gives great wealth.
Gaja Kesari Yoga — Jupiter in kendra from Moon. Gives wisdom, fame, respect.
Budhaditya Yoga — Sun and Mercury together. Gives intelligence and communication skills.
Hamsa Yoga — Jupiter in own/exalted sign in kendra. Makes person wise, spiritual, respected.
Malavya Yoga — Venus in own/exalted sign in kendra. Gives beauty, luxury, artistic talent.
Ruchaka Yoga — Mars in own/exalted sign in kendra. Gives courage, military success.
Bhadra Yoga — Mercury in own/exalted sign in kendra. Gives sharp intellect, business success.
Shasha Yoga — Saturn in own/exalted sign in kendra. Gives discipline, leadership, longevity.

Challenging Yogas:

Kemadruma Yoga — No planets adjacent to Moon. Causes mental instability, loneliness.
Grahan Yoga — Sun or Moon with Rahu/Ketu. Causes confusion, identity issues.
Shakata Yoga — Jupiter and Moon in 6/8 relationship. Causes fluctuating fortune.
Vish Yoga — Saturn and Moon together. Causes depression, pessimism.

STEP 13 — KUNDALI MATCHING (GUNA MILAN):
For marriage compatibility, 36 qualities (Gunas) are matched:

Varna (1 point) — spiritual compatibility, caste of soul
Vashya (2 points) — control and attraction between partners
Tara (3 points) — birth star compatibility, health and longevity
Yoni (4 points) — sexual and physical compatibility (14 animal symbols)
Graha Maitri (5 points) — mental compatibility, planetary friendship
Gana (6 points) — temperament match (Deva/Manushya/Rakshasa)
Bhakoot (7 points) — emotional and financial compatibility
Nadi (8 points) — health and genetic compatibility (most important)

Scoring:

0-17 points: Not recommended for marriage
18-24 points: Average match, marriage possible
25-32 points: Good match, happy marriage likely
33-36 points: Excellent match, very auspicious

NADI DOSHA — If both partners have same Nadi, it scores 0/8 and is considered a serious dosha (flaw). Remedies required.
MANGAL DOSHA — If Mars is in 1st, 2nd, 4th, 7th, 8th or 12th house, the person is "Manglik." Marriage to non-Manglik can cause problems. Both being Manglik cancels the dosha.
